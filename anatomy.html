The Anatomy of a Card

Number or Value

This is the rank printed on the card (2–10, Ace, & face cards assigned values 11–13). It appears in each corner and identifies the card’s game-value.

Suit and Pips

The suit (clubs, hearts, spades, diamonds) and its pips (small suit symbols) show card identity. Each numbered card has the same number of pips in the center.

Face

These are the “face” or “court” cards (Jack, Queen, King) which replace a number with a picture. There are 12 such cards in a standard deck.

Joker

Likely invented in the United States during the 1860s by players of Euchre, who added an extra trump, originally dubbed the “Best Bower” or “Little Joker.”

Back Design

The design on the reverse side of each card, typically uniform throughout a deck. It’s a strong brand/identity marker (common colours: red or blue).


Finish

A protective coating applied to the card’s front and back. Finish methods include varnish or lamination. This influences durability, handling (fan, shuffle), and performance suitability.

Stock

The material (“card stock”) the card is printed on—typically heavy paper (~300 GSM), plastic, or novelty wood/metal. The stock affects feel, durability, and cost.
